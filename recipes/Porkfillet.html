Pork fillet stew with mushroom

Pork la file a la mushroom

Sometimes just a classic will do! Then a good stew with pork fillet is a safe
choice. Our pork fillet stew with mushrooms is creamy and cheesy - just what
you can long for after a tired weekday evening. Serve piping hot with rice or
potatoes.

    Ingredients
  • 500 g pork fillet or pork tenderloin
  • 2 tablespoons butter
  • salt
  • pepper
  • 1 yellow onion
  • 250 g mushrooms
  • 2 tsp tomato puree
  • 1 chicken stock cube
  • 3 dl whipped cream
  • 2 dl milk
  • 2 tablespoons Japanese soy
  • 2 tsp Dijon mustard
  • 1 tsp corn starch

    • For serving
  • boiled rice or potatoes
  • salad
    Do this
  1. Cut the pork fillet into strips and brown in half the butter and season
    with salt and pepper. Place on a plate.
  2. Peel and chop the onion. Trim and divide the mushroom. Brown onions and
    mushrooms in a large pot in the rest of the butter. Add the tomato puree
    towards the end and let it brown for a minute while stirring.
  3. Crumble the bouillon cube and stir in the cream and milk, let it boil.
    Season with soy and mustard, salt and pepper. Stir the cornstarch into a
    little water and stir. Turn the meat into the pot and let it heat up.
  4. To serve: Serve the stew with rice or boiled potatoes and a salad.
